🎯 Key Insights

The Math That Matters

  • • $190K in NJ ≠ $190K in Dublin — Your money goes further abroad due to social benefits
  • • Childcare is the biggest swing: $30K+ savings in NL/CA vs US
  • • Healthcare removes bankruptcy risk — priceless for family planning
  • • Property tax savings: $12K/yr in Ireland vs NJ

Hidden Value Not in Numbers

  • • 5-6 weeks vacation vs 2-3 weeks in US
  • • Parental leave: 6-12 months vs 0-12 weeks
  • • Job security: Much harder to fire workers in EU
  • • Political stability: The whole reason you're doing this

✅ Recommendation

Based purely on financial equivalency:

  1. Netherlands (with 30% ruling) — Lowest gross income needed, massive childcare savings, but citizenship path uncertain
  2. Ireland — Your projected income exceeds target, fastest EU citizenship (5 years), military neutral
  3. Canada — Slightly below target income, but $10/day childcare closes the gap. Proximity to US is the concern.

All three options are financially viable for your family. The decision should be based on non-financial factors: political stability, citizenship timeline, and distance from US.
