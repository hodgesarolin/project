## Russia Response

### Putin's Statements on Trump's Return

#### January 2025: Initial Optimism
- Putin's first public comments following Trump's inauguration indicated he sees a second Trump presidency as an opportunity for a "new era in U.S.-Russian relations" [Source](https://abcnews.go.com/International/putin-eyes-reset-us-russia-relations/story?id=117931770)
- Putin praised Trump saying he "showed courage to win in a convincing manner"
- Recognized "the desire [of Trump's team] to restore direct contacts with Russia" while blaming the Biden administration for the rupture in relations
- Putin welcomed Trump's statements about "the need to do everything possible to prevent World War III"

#### February 2025: First Direct Communications
- Trump announced he spoke to Putin by phone about ending the war in Ukraine
- Trump wrote on Truth Social: "As we both agreed, we want to stop the millions of deaths taking place in the War with Russia/Ukraine" [Source](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/white-house/trump-says-spoke-putin-ending-war-ukraine-rcna191882)
- Both agreed to "work together, very closely, including visiting each other's Nations"

#### July 2025: Stalling Progress
- Putin told Trump that Moscow "will not back down" on its goal of eliminating the "root causes" of the war in Ukraine [Source](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/7/3/russia-will-not-back-down-on-ukraine-war-goals-putin-tells-trump)
- Trump admitted he had made "no progress" with Putin on moving towards a ceasefire after their sixth phone call
- Putin rejected Washington's proposal for an unconditional ceasefire

#### August 2025: Alaska Summit
- Putin praised Trump before the Alaska summit, saying the administration "is making quite an energetic and sincere effort, in my opinion, to stop hostilities" [Source](https://abcnews.go.com/International/zelenskyy-rallies-trump-european-allies-setting-red-lines/story?id=124637288)

#### December 2025: Hardline Position Maintained
- Following talks in Moscow with Trump's envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner, Putin said some proposals in the U.S. plan were "unacceptable" [Source](https://www.cnn.com/2025/12/03/europe/putin-trump-ukraine-war-analysis-intl)
- At his year-end press conference, Putin told NBC News "the ball was in Ukraine and the West's court" showing no sign of compromise [Source](https://www.nbcnews.com/world/russia/live-blog/live-updates-vladimir-putin-news-conference-russia-ukraine-trump-peace-rcna249474)
- Russia refused to drop its demand that Ukraine completely withdraw from Donetsk and Luhansk regions
- Putin voiced optimism that Russian forces would seize control of all four eastern Ukrainian regions he claimed

### Ukraine War Developments

#### US Policy Shifts on Ukraine Aid

**March 2025: Initial Aid Pause**
- Following disagreements during the Trump-Zelenskyy meeting, the administration paused all military aid to Ukraine on March 3, 2025 [Source](https://www.cnn.com/2025/03/03/politics/trump-administration-ukraine-aid)
- Officials warned this could have "dire effect on Ukraine's war-fighting abilities"

**Key Policy Changes Under Trump Administration:**
- Trump adopted a significantly different approach from Biden, prioritizing a negotiated peace agreement
- Did not seek congressional approval for any new funding for US military assistance
- Stepped back from leading the Ukraine Defence Contact Group [Source](https://commonslibrary.parliament.uk/research-briefings/cbp-10308/)

**July 2025: Suspension and Resumption**
- US administration confirmed some military aid was being suspended during a Department of Defense capability review
- Suspended aid reportedly included Patriot air defense missiles and precision guided weapons
- Aid resumed days later at President Trump's direction

**PURL Mechanism (July-September 2025)**
- On July 14, 2025, Trump announced the Prioritized Ukraine Requirements List (PURL) mechanism [Source](https://www.npr.org/2025/07/15/nx-s1-5468458/ukraine-military-aid-trump)
- Trump made clear the US will not pay for this new assistance
- European allies purchase American arms for Ukraine under this mechanism
- Two $500 million shipments authorized
- Countries pledging funds: Canada, Germany, Sweden, Denmark, Norway, Netherlands
- Total raised by September 6: $2 billion

**NDAA 2025 Provisions:**
- December 17: Senate passed the annual National Defense Authorization Act
- Includes $400 million in USAI funding for 2026 and 2027 - a sharp decrease from the April 2024 Ukraine supplemental's nearly $14 billion [Source](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/blogs/ukrainealert/whats-in-the-new-us-defense-bill-for-ukraine/)

**November 2025: Trump's Peace Plan Proposal**
- Trump proposed a plan requiring Ukraine to give up territory including major cities
- Would limit Ukraine's troop size and bar NATO membership
- Would grant Ukraine and Russia's leaders amnesty
- Allow Ukraine to pursue EU membership
- Pay for a US security guarantee
- Receive $100 billion in redistributed Russian assets

### Sanctions Changes

**First 9 Months: No New Sanctions**
- Trump administration did not remove or relax main sanctions against Russia [Source](https://commonslibrary.parliament.uk/research-briefings/cbp-10342/)
- However, did not join UK, EU and allies in imposing any new sanctions
- July 2025: US did not support lowering the Oil Price Cap

**October 2025: First Direct Sanctions Under Trump**
- US imposed direct sanctions on Russia for the first time under Trump administration [Source](https://www.cnn.com/2025/10/22/politics/bessent-sanctions-russia)
- Treasury sanctioned Russia's two largest oil companies: Rosneft and Lukoil
- Treasury stated sanctions were due to Russia's "lack of serious commitment to a peace process"
- 30 subsidiaries of Rosneft and Lukoil also sanctioned
- Coordinated with UK (October 15) and EU's 19th Russia Sanctions Package (October 23)

**Sanctioning Russia Act of 2025**
- Trump greenlit bipartisan sanctions bill co-authored by Senators Graham and Blumenthal [Source](https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-greenlights-russian-sanctions-bill-paving-way-500-tariff-countries-supporting-moscow-graham)
- Would require 500% tariff on goods from any country purchasing Russian oil, petroleum, or uranium
- Targets countries like China and India that continue to purchase discounted Russian energy

### Trade War Escalation: Timeline

**February 2025**
- Trump signed Executive Order 14195 declaring national emergency regarding drug trafficking from China [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tariffs_in_the_second_Trump_administration)
- 10% tariffs on all imports from China took effect February 4, 2025

**March 2025**
- March 3: Trump raised levies on Chinese goods by another 10% to cumulative 20% [Source](https://www.axios.com/2025/03/03/ukraine-military-aid-us-trump)
- March 4: China retaliated with 15% tariff on chicken, wheat, corn, cotton; 10% on soybeans, pork, beef, etc.

**April 2025: "Liberation Day"**
- April 2: Trump raised tariffs on China by another 34% during "Liberation Day" speech [Source](https://www.weforum.org/stories/2025/06/trumps-us-china-trade-tariffs-timeline/)
- China's minimum tariff rate increased to 145%
- April 9: S&P 500 rose 9.52% on tariff suspension announcement - largest one-day gain since 2008
- April 21: Major US retailer CEOs warned of visible price increases and shortages within two weeks

**May 2025**
- US and China agreed to lower tariffs: China to 10% from 125%, US to 30% from 145%

**October 2025**
- Trump threatened additional 100% tax on Chinese imports [Source](https://www.cnn.com/2025/10/10/economy/trump-china-tariff-threats-economy)

**November 2025: Deal**
- US agreed to lower tariffs by 10 percentage points, effective November 10, 2025 [Source](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/11/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-strikes-deal-on-economic-and-trade-relations-with-china/)
- Heightened reciprocal tariffs suspended until November 10, 2026
- China agreed to suspend all retaliatory tariffs announced since March 4, 2025

**Economic Impact Statistics:**
- Average tax increase: $1,200 per US household in 2025 [Source](https://taxfoundation.org/research/all/federal/trump-tariffs-trade-war/)
- US economy shrank in Q1 2025
- China's goods trade surplus climbed past $1 trillion for first time
- US exports to China down approximately 40% year-on-year in Q3
- Chinese oil imports from US decreased by 90%

## Global Democratic Backsliding

### Freedom House: Freedom in the World 2025

**Key Statistics:**
- 19th consecutive year of global freedom decline [Source](https://freedomhouse.org/article/new-report-amid-unprecedented-wave-elections-political-violence-and-armed-conflict-fueled)
- 60 countries experienced deterioration in political rights and civil liberties
- Only 34 countries secured improvements
- Only 20% of world's people live in countries rated Free
- 40% live in countries rated Not Free
- 84 countries rated Free; 57 Partly Free; 54 Not Free
- Electoral democracies: 106 out of 195 (down from 110 previous year)

**Largest Score Declines:**
- El Salvador
- Haiti
- Kuwait
- Tunisia

**Largest Score Gains:**
- Bangladesh
- Bhutan
- Sri Lanka
- Syria

**Status Downgrades:**
- Kuwait, Niger, Tanzania, Thailand: fell from 'Partly Free' to 'Not Free' [Source](https://freedomhouse.org/sites/default/files/2025-02/FITW_World_2025_Feb.2025.pdf)

**Election Violence:**
- Of 66 countries/territories hosting national elections in 2024: 40% featured election-related violence
- Candidates attacked in at least 20 countries
- Polling places attacked in at least 14 countries

### V-Dem Democracy Report 2025

**Title:** "25 Years of Autocratization - Democracy Trumped?"

**Key Findings:**
- As of 2024: 91 autocracies vs 88 democracies globally [Source](https://www.v-dem.net/documents/60/V-dem-dr__2025_lowres.pdf)
- Liberal democracies: only 29 worldwide (least common regime type)
- 72% of world's population now lives in autocracies
- 45 countries in ongoing episodes of autocratization
- Only 19 countries in democratization episodes
- 25 of autocratizing countries were democracies at start of decline; only 9 remain democracies

**Regional Trends:**
- Eastern Europe and South and Central Asia in "particularly steep decline" [Source](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/13510347.2025.2487825)
- North America and Western Europe democracy levels fell to 1983 levels

**Specific Countries:**
- Transitions to Closed Autocracy: Belarus, Gabon, Lebanon, Niger
- Watchlist countries: Cyprus, Slovakia, Slovenia
- Greece: registered as "statistically confirmed autocratizer" with steep decline
- Latin America regressing: Argentina, El Salvador, Guyana, Haiti, Mexico, Nicaragua, Peru
- United States: identified as undergoing "fastest evolving episode of autocratization the USA has been through in modern history" [Source](https://www.democracywithoutborders.org/36317/autocracies-outnumber-democracies-for-the-first-time-in-20-years-v-dem/)

**Democratizing Countries:**
- Bolivia, Brazil, Ecuador, Dominican Republic, Honduras
- First three "successfully reversing autocratization processes"

## International Institutions

### WHO Withdrawal

**Executive Order 14155 (January 20, 2025):**
- Signed on first day of Trump's second term [Source](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/withdrawing-the-united-states-from-the-worldhealth-organization/)
- Cited: mishandling of COVID-19 pandemic, failure to adopt reforms, political influence concerns
- Noted China (1.4 billion population) contributes nearly 90% less than US to WHO

**Actions:**
- Paused all planned transfers of US funds to WHO
- Recalled and reassigned US personnel working with WHO
- Ceased negotiations on WHO Pandemic Agreement

**Effective Date:**
- US withdrawal effective January 22, 2026
- US contributed $261 million (18% of WHO funding) in 2024-2025 [Source](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12089832/)

**Reactions:**
- WHO issued statement expressing regret, urging reconsideration [Source](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMp2501790)
- WHO implemented budget cuts, recruitment freeze, travel reductions
- Public health experts warned of "catastrophic effects on both domestic and global health"
