## First Impeachment

### Timeline of Events

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| July 18, 2019 | State Department and Pentagon informed Trump was withholding $392 million in military aid to Ukraine |
| **July 25, 2019** | **Trump-Zelensky phone call** |
| August 2019 | Whistleblower complaint filed |
| September 11, 2019 | Administration quietly lifted hold on Ukraine aid under Congressional pressure |
| September 24, 2019 | Speaker Pelosi announced formal impeachment inquiry |
| September 25, 2019 | White House released partial call transcript |
| September 26, 2019 | Whistleblower complaint publicly released |
| November 13-21, 2019 | Public testimony before House Intelligence Committee |
| **December 18, 2019** | **House impeached Trump on two articles** |
| **February 5, 2020** | **Senate acquitted Trump** |

### The Phone Call

On **July 25, 2019**, President Trump spoke with newly-elected Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky. During the call, Trump asked Ukraine to investigate former Vice President Joe Biden and his son Hunter.

**Key Quote from Call Transcript:**
> "I would like you to do us a favor though..."

Trump later described the call as "perfect" on numerous occasions.

### Articles of Impeachment

1. **Abuse of Power** - Soliciting foreign interference in the 2020 election
2. **Obstruction of Congress** - Blocking witnesses and documents

### Key Testimony

**Gordon Sondland** (U.S. Ambassador to the European Union):
> Testified he worked with Giuliani at Trump's "express direction" to arrange a quid pro quo with Ukraine

**Bill Taylor** (Top U.S. diplomat to Ukraine):
- Testified that military aid and a White House meeting were conditioned on Ukraine publicly announcing investigations into the Bidens

### Government Accountability Office Finding

In **January 2020**, the nonpartisan GAO concluded that **the White House broke federal law** by withholding congressionally approved military aid to Ukraine.

### Senate Acquittal (February 5, 2020)

| Article | Vote | Result |
|---------|------|--------|
| Abuse of Power | 52-48 | Acquitted |
| Obstruction of Congress | 53-47 | Acquitted |

**Historic Note:** Senator Mitt Romney (R-UT) became the **first senator in American history** to vote to convict a president of his own party in an impeachment trial.

**Romney's statement:**
> "Corrupting an election to keep oneself in office is perhaps the most abusive and destructive violation of one's oath of office that I can imagine."

## Government Shutdown

### Key Facts

| Metric | Data |
|--------|------|
| Duration | **35 days** (December 22, 2018 - January 25, 2019) |
| Record Status | **Longest government shutdown in U.S. history** |
| Cause | Impasse over $5.7 billion for border wall |
| Workers Furloughed | 380,000 |
| Workers Required to Work Without Pay | 420,000 |
| Economic Cost | **$3 billion in lost GDP** (CBO estimate) |

### Timeline

- **December 22, 2018:** Shutdown began when Democrats rejected a continuing resolution with border wall funding
- **January 12, 2019:** Became the longest shutdown in U.S. history (surpassing 21-day shutdown of 1995-1996)
- **January 25, 2019:** Ended after FAA forced to limit flights due to air traffic controller sick-outs

### Resolution

The shutdown ended with a temporary funding bill that **did not include border wall money** - the very thing Trump had demanded.

### Impact
- Air traffic controllers, TSA agents, and other essential workers were required to work without pay
- Food safety inspections reduced
- National parks damaged
- IRS refunds delayed

## Mueller Report

### Overview

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| Official Title | "Report On The Investigation Into Russian Interference In The 2016 Presidential Election" |
| Submitted | March 22, 2019 |
| Public Release | April 18, 2019 (redacted) |
| Length | 448 pages |
| Indictments | 37 |
| Guilty Pleas/Convictions | 7 |

### Key Findings

#### On Russian Interference
> "The Russian government interfered in the 2016 presidential election in sweeping and systematic fashion."

Methods included:
- Internet Research Agency disinformation campaigns
- Social media operations supporting Trump and disparaging Clinton
- Hacking operations to gather and disseminate information

#### On Conspiracy/Coordination
> The investigation "did not establish that members of the Trump campaign conspired or coordinated with the Russian government in its election interference activities."

However, the report noted:
- Russian interference was **welcomed** by the Trump campaign
- Multiple links existed between campaign and Russian officials
- Several campaign associates made **false statements** about contacts

#### On Obstruction of Justice

The report examined **10 instances** where Trump may have obstructed justice.

**Critical Quote:**
> "While this report does not conclude that the President committed a crime, it also does not exonerate him."

**Key Obstruction Incidents:**
1. **June 2017:** Trump directed Don McGahn to order Mueller's firing; later asked McGahn to falsely deny this (McGahn refused)
2. **February 2017:** After firing Flynn, Trump cleared his office for one-on-one meeting with FBI Director Comey and asked him to "let [Flynn] go"

#### Why No Indictment Decision
Mueller cited a 2000 Office of Legal Counsel opinion that a sitting president cannot be indicted or criminally prosecuted.

**July 2019 Congressional Testimony:**
Mueller stated a president could be charged with crimes including obstruction of justice **after leaving office**.

## Send Her Back Rally

### Event Details

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| Date | **July 17, 2019** |
| Location | Greenville, North Carolina |
| Target | Rep. Ilhan Omar (D-MN) |

### What Happened

During a campaign rally, as Trump attacked Rep. Ilhan Omar with accusations, the crowd began chanting **"Send her back!"**

**Duration:** Trump paused and listened to the chant for **13 seconds** as it grew louder, making no effort to stop it.

### Context

- Omar is one of four progressive Democratic congresswomen (known as "The Squad") whom Trump had told to **"go back"** to the "places from which they came" on Twitter days earlier
- Omar was **born in Somalia** and became a U.S. citizen as a teenager
- She was elected to Congress in 2018

### Trump's Contradictory Response

**July 18, 2019 (next day):**
> "I was not happy with it - I disagree with it."

When asked why he didn't stop it:
> "I think I did."

**Reality:** Video shows Trump did not stop or discourage the chant; he paused to allow it to continue.

### Omar's Response

> "We have condemned his racist remarks. I believe he is fascist."

> "We are Americans as much as everyone else. This is our country. And we are where we belong."

### Expert Assessment

The chant was widely condemned as racist and echoing xenophobic rhetoric targeting immigrants and naturalized citizens.

## Greenland Purchase Attempt

### Timeline

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| August 2019 | Reports emerge Trump discussed purchasing Greenland |
| August 18, 2019 | Trump confirms interest but says "not No. 1 on the burner" |
| August 18, 2019 | Danish PM Frederiksen rejects idea as "absurd" |
| August 20, 2019 | Trump cancels state visit to Denmark |
| August 21, 2019 | Trump calls Frederiksen's response "nasty" |

### Danish Response

**Prime Minister Mette Frederiksen:**
> "Greenland is not for sale. Greenland is not Danish. Greenland is Greenlandic. I strongly hope that this is not meant seriously."

**Premier of Greenland Kim Kielsen** and multiple Danish officials called the idea "completely ridiculous."

### Trump's Reaction

After Frederiksen's rejection:
> "I thought the Prime Minister's statement that it was an absurd idea was nasty, it was an inappropriate statement, all she had to do was say 'no we wouldn't be interested.'"

Trump cancelled his planned state visit to Denmark, calling Frederiksen's response "inappropriate."

### Frederiksen's Response to Cancellation

> "It is with regret and surprise that I received the news that President Trump has canceled his state visit to Denmark on the 2nd and 3rd of September."

### Historical Note

This was not the first time the U.S. considered purchasing Greenland - the Truman administration explored the idea in 1946.

## Syria Withdrawal and Kurdish Betrayal

### Timeline

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **October 6, 2019** | Trump announced immediate withdrawal of U.S. troops from Kurdish-controlled northeastern Syria after phone call with Turkish President Erdogan |
| **October 9, 2019** | Turkey launched military offensive into the region |
| October 12, 2019 | Kurdish political leader Hevrin Khalaf murdered along with civilians |
| October 2019 | U.S. announced sanctions on Turkish officials |
| Late October 2019 | Kurds agreed to withdraw from 20 miles of Turkish border |

### Background

The Kurds had been vital U.S. allies in the fight against ISIS:
- Liberated ISIS capital of Raqqa
- **March 2019:** Defeated ISIS in Baghouz, destroying its last sanctuary in Syria
- Controlled approximately 100,000 ISIS-affiliated prisoners

### The Decision

Following a phone call with Turkish President Erdogan, Trump endorsed Turkish military plans and ordered withdrawal of U.S. troops that had been protecting Kurdish forces.

### Consequences

**Civilian Deaths:**
- Kurdish political leader Hevrin Khalaf, her driver, an aide, and at least 8 more civilians were murdered on the M4 highway
- Amnesty International declared the killings and attacks **war crimes**

**ISIS Prisoners:**
- ~800 ISIS prisoners reportedly escaped from camps controlled by Kurdish fighters

**Ethnic Cleansing Concerns:**
- Reports of war crimes by Turkish-backed militias
- Widespread panic over possible ethnic cleansing

### Military Response

**U.S. Special Forces soldier in Syria:**
> "It's pretty messed up what's happening out here."

**Senior U.S. defense official:**
> The Kurdish-led Syrian Democratic Forces "are fighting a force that intends to eliminate their people because we green lighted their operation."

**Another U.S. military official:**
> Said he was "ashamed" of his country's actions

### Kurdish Response

- Protesters jeered American convoys and pelted them with rotten vegetables and stones
- Signs read: **"Thanks for U.S. people, but Trump betrayed us."**

### Republican Criticism

| Official | Response |
|----------|----------|
| Sen. Lindsey Graham | Announced bipartisan sanctions bill |
| Rep. Adam Kinzinger | Called it a "grave mistake" |
| Sen. Marco Rubio | Strongly opposed the move |
| Nikki Haley (former UN Ambassador) | Called it a "catastrophe" |

Several Trump appointees held the **shortest service tenures in history** for their positions:

### The "Acting" Officials Problem

Trump used acting appointees to avoid Senate confirmation for many positions.

- Had harder time accomplishing their work
- Had less influence within the administration

**From Mattis's resignation letter:**
> "Because you have the right to have a Secretary of Defense whose views are better aligned with yours, I believe it is right for me to step down from my position."

His resignation came days after Trump's Syria withdrawal decision.

## Hydroxychloroquine Controversy

### Timeline

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Mid-March 2020 | Trump begins promoting hydroxychloroquine |
| March 28, 2020 | FDA grants Emergency Use Authorization |
| March-April 2020 | Trump makes 11 tweets, 65 briefing mentions about HCQ |
| **April 24, 2020** | **FDA warns against HCQ use outside hospitals/trials** |
| June 2020 | Major studies show no benefit |
| **June 15, 2020** | **FDA revokes Emergency Use Authorization** |

### Trump's Promotion

From March 1 to April 30, 2020, Trump:
- Made **11 tweets** about hydroxychloroquine/chloroquine
- Mentioned these drugs **65 times** in White House briefings

He called them potential "game changers."

### FDA Warning (April 24, 2020)

The FDA issued a drug safety communication warning against hydroxychloroquine use to treat COVID-19 outside hospitals or clinical trials.

**FDA Statement:**
> "Hydroxychloroquine and chloroquine can cause abnormal heart rhythms such as QT interval prolongation and ventricular tachycardia. These risks may increase when these medicines are combined with other medicines known to prolong the QT interval, including azithromycin."

### Scientific Studies

**Recovery Trial (June 2020):**
- No significant benefit for COVID-19 patients
- No reduction in mortality rates

**NIH:**
- Abandoned clinical trials after finding HCQ "was very unlikely to be beneficial to hospitalized patients with COVID-19"
- Statement: "Study shows treatment does no harm, but provides no benefit"

### FDA Revokes Authorization (June 15, 2020)

**FDA Statement:**
> "FDA has determined that CQ and HCQ are unlikely to be effective in treating COVID-19 for the authorized uses in the EUA. Additionally, in light of ongoing serious cardiac adverse events and other serious side effects, the known and potential benefits of CQ and HCQ no longer outweigh the known and potential risks."

### Whistleblower Complaint

**Rick Bright** (former BARDA director) stated in his whistleblower complaint that he:
> "resisted efforts to fall into line with the Administration's directive to promote the broad use of chloroquine and hydroxychloroquine and to award lucrative contracts for these and other drugs even though they lacked scientific merit."

## Disinfectant Comments

### Date and Context

**April 23, 2020** - White House Coronavirus Task Force Briefing

William Bryan, DHS undersecretary, had just presented research showing that sun exposure and cleaning agents like bleach have adverse effects on the coronavirus.

### Trump's Exact Quote

> "And then I see the disinfectant, where it knocks it out in one minute. And is there a way we can do something like that, by injection inside or almost a cleaning, because you see it gets in the lungs and it does a tremendous number on the lungs, so it'd be interesting to check that, so that you're going to have to use medical doctors with, but it sounds interesting to me."

### Clarification (4 minutes later)

When asked by a reporter about injecting cleaning products:
> "It wouldn't be through injection. We're talking about through almost a cleaning, sterilization of an area. Maybe it works, maybe it doesn't work."

### "Sarcasm" Claim (April 24, 2020)

The next day, Trump claimed:
> "No, I was asking a question sarcastically to reporters like you, just to see what would happen."

### Fact-Check Analysis

**What Trump did NOT do:**
- He did not explicitly tell people to inject bleach

**What Trump DID do:**
- Suggested, in a public briefing, that injecting disinfectants might be explored as a treatment
- Asked government health officials to look into it

### Corporate Response

**Reckitt Benckiser (maker of Lysol and Dettol):**
> "We must be clear that under no circumstance should our disinfectant products be administered into the human body (through injection, ingestion or any other route)."

## PPE Shortage Crisis

### The Shortage

In spring 2020, healthcare workers faced critical shortages of:
- N95 masks
- Surgical masks
- Gowns
- Gloves
- Ventilators

### Strategic National Stockpile (Pre-Pandemic)

| Item | Stockpile Quantity |
|------|-------------------|
| N95 masks | 12 million |
| Surgical/procedural masks | 30 million |
| Gowns | 4 million |
| Gloves | 16 million |

### Trump's Approach

**March 13, 2020 (after declaring national emergency):**
Trump directed governors to order their own supplies, saying the federal government is **"not a shipping clerk."**

### States Bidding Against Each Other

**Gov. Andrew Cuomo (D-NY):**
> "The price of ventilators went from $25,000 to 45,000 because states kept outbidding each other. We bid $25,000. California says, 'I'll give you $30,000' and Illinois says, 'I'll give you $35,000' and Florida says 'I'll give you $40,000.' We're literally bidding up the prices ourselves."

Cuomo compared it to competing on eBay with 50 other states and FEMA.

**Gov. Charlie Baker (R-MA):**
Told Trump he followed the directive to buy supplies but "on three big orders, we lost" to the federal government.

### Louisiana Example

Louisiana requested **10+ million units** of PPE from the Strategic National Stockpile.
They received approximately **13%** of their request.

### Bipartisan Criticism

**Gov. Gretchen Whitmer (D-MI) and Gov. Larry Hogan (R-MD):**
> "The lack of any centralized coordination is creating a counterproductive competition between states and the federal government to secure limited supplies, driving up prices and exacerbating existing shortages."

**Gov. Jay Inslee (D-WA):**
States had been forced into "scrambling around the world" to purchase PPE "because the federal government would not accept the responsibility."

### Defense Production Act

Trump invoked the Defense Production Act but made only **sparing use** of its authorities.
- Experts said efforts started far too late
- Only about half the masks ordered would be delivered by end of 2020
