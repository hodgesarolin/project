# Nazi-Era Rhetoric, Slogans, and Imagery in Trump Administration

**Created:** 2026-01-14
**Status:** Active tracking
**Pattern:** Increasing use of Nazi-era phrases, white nationalist songs, and fascist-associated imagery in official government communications

---

## Summary

Since January 2025, the Trump administration has repeatedly used language, slogans, songs, fonts, and imagery that echo Nazi Germany, fascist movements, and contemporary white nationalist groups. While individual incidents have been defended as coincidence or overreaction, the cumulative pattern has drawn alarm from historians, civil rights organizations, and international observers.

---

## Documented Incidents

### January 2026 - DHS "We'll Have Our Home Again" (Jan 11)

**What happened:** Less than two days after ICE agent Jonathan Ross fatally shot Renee Good in Minneapolis, DHS's official Instagram account posted a recruitment message proclaiming "We'll Have Our Home Again," attaching a song of the same name.

**Origin of phrase:**
- Originally written by the **Männerbund**, a nationalist group associated with Germany's Völkisch movement
- The song has been adopted by white nationalist spaces online
- Proud Boys members have been recorded chanting "By God, we'll have our home" at rallies
- Lyrics about reclaiming "our home" by "blood or sweat" - language used in white nationalist calls for race war
- **Opened the manifesto** of Ryan Christopher Palmeter, the 21-year-old white supremacist who killed three Black people in Jacksonville, FL in 2023

**Expert reaction:**
- Historian Adam Tooze: "US government agency tweeting out lyrics from a white nationalist anthem popular in Mannerbund circles" (930,000 views)
- SPLC documented the pattern in a Hatewatch investigation

**DHS response:** "Calling everything you dislike 'Nazi propaganda' is tiresome."

**Sources:** [The Intercept](https://theintercept.com/2026/01/13/dhs-ice-white-nationalist-neo-nazi/), [Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/trump-administration-ice-dhs-post-lyrics-criticized-11346700), [SPLC](https://www.splcenter.org/resources/hatewatch/dhs-white-nationalist-anti-immigrant-social-media/)

### January 2026 - Department of Labor "One Homeland" Post (Jan 11)

**What happened:** U.S. Department of Labor posted 11-second video with caption: "One Homeland. One People. One Heritage. Remember who you are, American."

**Nazi parallel:**
- Critics immediately noted similarity to **"Ein Volk, ein Reich, ein Führer"** ("One People, One Realm, One Leader")
- The Nazi slogan was central to Third Reich propaganda

**Reach:** Viewed over 10 million times

**Sources:** [Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/trump-administration-accused-echoing-nazi-slogans-11353285), [Truthout](https://truthout.org/articles/why-is-the-department-of-labor-posting-white-nationalist-propaganda/)

---

### January 2026 - DHS Podium "One of Ours All of Yours" (Jan 8)

**What happened:** At Kristi Noem's press conference following Renee Good's killing, DHS podium displayed: "One of Ours All of Yours"

**Claimed parallels:**
- Critics linked to **Lidice Massacre (1942)** - Nazi collective reprisal where entire Czech village was destroyed
- More directly traced to Spanish Civil War Franco slogan: **"Uno de los nuestros, todos de los vuestros"** - used to justify collective punishment

**Fact-check status:** No verified Nazi origin for exact phrase, but evokes collective reprisal doctrine that Nazis practiced

**Reaction:** Tom Morello (Rage Against the Machine) called it out as Nazi slogan

**Sources:** [Lead Stories Fact Check](https://leadstories.com/hoax-alert/2026/01/fact-check-one-of-ours-all-of-yours-is-not-a-phrase-from-nazi-germany-however-it-was-really-displayed-on-a-lectern-at-a-dhs-press-conference-in-new-york-city.html), [PrimeTimer](https://www.primetimer.com/features/did-kristi-noem-really-say-one-of-ours-all-of-yours-history-of-controversial-slogan-explored-post-press-conference-podium-message)

### January 2026 - DHS "Which Way, American Man?" Post

**What happened:** DHS shared post with caption "Which way, American man?"

**Origin:** References **"Which Way Western Man?"** - 1978 book by avowed white supremacist William Gayley Simpson, who argued Hitler was right and led the National Alliance (American neo-Nazi organization)

**Sources:** [IBTimes](https://www.ibtimes.co.uk/dhs-faces-outcry-over-alleged-use-nazi-style-typography-official-posts-1770901)

### January 2026 - Blackletter/Fraktur Font in ICE Recruitment

**What happened:** DHS/ICE recruitment materials using bold Old English-inspired Blackletter typography with slogans like "Protect. Serve. Deport."

**Historical context:**
- Fraktur font was used on cover of Hitler's **Mein Kampf** and early Nazi propaganda
- Typography instructor Khara Cloutier (CalArts): comparison is "absolutely relevant"
- Design critic Steven Heller: "probably more gangsta than Nazi" but "clearly using the wrong typeface"

**Counterpoint:** Nazis actually banned Fraktur in 1941, calling it "Jewish-influenced"

**DHS response:** "Go outside, touch grass, and get a grip."

**Sources:** [HuffPost](https://www.huffpost.com/entry/nazi-font-in-ice-recruitment-clip_l_689b7a07e4b0196f49c523d7), [Joe.My.God](https://www.joemygod.com/2026/01/dhs-continues-spree-of-nazi-slogans-and-even-fonts/)

### January 2025 - Elon Musk Salute at Inauguration (Jan 20)

**What happened:** At Trump inauguration rally, Elon Musk twice made a gesture interpreted by many as Nazi/fascist Roman salute - touching chest then extending arm upward

**Reactions:**
- **Condemned:** Rep. Jerry Nadler, multiple Jewish organizations, widespread international criticism (illegal gesture in Germany)
- **Celebrated:** Neo-Nazi and white supremacist groups celebrated it as Nazi salute
- **Defended:** ADL initially called it "awkward gesture in a moment of enthusiasm"

**Musk's response:** Dismissed as "dirty tricks" and "tired" attack; later made Nazi-themed puns on social media (which ADL called "offensive")

**Sources:** [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elon_Musk_salute_controversy), [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/01/23/nx-s1-5269719/elon-musk-salute-inauguration-day-nazis), [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/1/21/elon-musk-at-trumps-inauguration-whats-the-history-of-the-nazi-salute)

## Recurring Rhetoric (2023-2026)

### "Poisoning the Blood"

**Usage:** Trump used phrase at least four times in 2023 campaign, continued into administration

**Nazi parallel:** Hitler in Mein Kampf: "All great cultures of the past perished only because the originally creative race died out from blood poisoning"

**Expert analysis:**
- Brian Levin (Center for Study of Hate and Extremism): "The whole notion of blood and soil is taken directly from Hitler"
- Ruth Ben-Ghiat (NYU historian of fascism): "The Nazis made the fear of blood pollution...a foundation of their state"

**Stephen Miller's role:** Backed up Trump's comments, called for "massive large-scale deportations by the millions"

**Sources:** [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2023/12/30/trump-poisoning-the-blood-racism), [MSNBC](https://www.ms.now/opinion/msnbc-opinion/trump-immigrant-comments-hitler-mein-kampf-poisoning-blood-rcna130251)

---

### "Vermin" / "Animals"

**Usage:** Trump called political opponents "vermin"; at December 2025 Cabinet meeting called immigrants "animals in many cases"

**Nazi parallel:** Dehumanizing language central to Nazi propaganda - Jews depicted as rats, vermin

**Sources:** [PBS](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/show/how-trumps-rhetoric-compares-to-historic-fascist-language), [Harvard Political Review](https://theharvardpoliticalreview.com/trump-rhetoric-hitler/)

---

### "Enemy of the People"

**Usage:** Repeated attacks on press as "enemy of the people"

**Historical use:** Stalin's term ("vrag naroda"); also used in Nazi Germany

**Sources:** [PBS](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/with-gestapo-comment-trump-adds-to-numerous-past-nazi-germany-references)
