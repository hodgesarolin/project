# Philippines Under Rodrigo Duterte (2016-2022)

## Executive Summary

Rodrigo Duterte's presidency (2016-2022) represents a dramatic case of democratic backsliding in Southeast Asia. Rising from his reputation as the tough-talking "Punisher" mayor of Davao City, Duterte won the 2016 election on promises to eliminate crime and corruption "within three to six months." His administration was defined by a brutal "war on drugs" that resulted in an estimated 12,000-30,000 extrajudicial killings, systematic attacks on press freedom, the detention of political opponents, and a controversial pivot toward China. In March 2025, Duterte became the first Asian former head of state arrested and surrendered to the International Criminal Court, where he faces charges of crimes against humanity. His legacy remains contested, with his daughter Sara serving as Vice President until her impeachment proceedings in 2025.

## I. Pre-Conditions: The Crisis That Enabled Duterte's Rise

### A. Post-Marcos Democratic Stagnation

The Philippines' democratic transition following the 1986 "People Power Revolution" against Ferdinand Marcos failed to deliver on promises of reform. [Source: [Carnegie Endowment](https://carnegieendowment.org/research/2019/01/philippine-politics-under-duterte-a-midterm-assessment)]

**Structural Problems:**
- Oligarchic structures dominated both political and economic spheres
- Enduring dominance of family clans, including the Marcos family
- Corruption remained endemic across all levels of government
- 2024 Corruption Perceptions Index: 33 out of 100 [Source: [Wikipedia - Corruption in the Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corruption_in_the_Philippines)]

**The Aquino III Administration (2010-2016):**
- Achieved highest economic growth rates in modern Philippine history
- Anti-corruption drive produced notable prosecutions
- However, growth benefits concentrated among elites: 40 families captured 76% of growth benefits
- 25 million Filipinos continued earning less than $1 per day
- Neoliberal policies exacerbated inequality [Source: [Focus on the Global South](https://focusweb.org/the-marcos-duterte-dynastic-regime-in-the-philippines-how-long-will-it-last/)]

### B. Crime and Drug Concerns

Crime has been a recurring political issue in the Philippines since the 1960s. Marcos used criminality to justify martial law in 1972, and Joseph Estrada's crime-fighting image fueled his 1998 election victory. [Source: [Brookings](https://www.brookings.edu/articles/duterte-democracy-and-defense/)]

**Crime as Political Issue:**
- High poverty levels contributed to persistent criminal activity
- Underresourced and corruption-prone law enforcement
- Glacially slow judiciary system
- Drug trafficking and illegal gambling as significant features of political economy [Source: [Brookings](https://www.brookings.edu/articles/duterte-democracy-and-defense/)]

**Anti-Liberal Sentiment:**
- Liberal reformism criticized for failing to establish participatory governance
- Human rights discourse portrayed as contradictory to peace, order, and development
- Growing frustration with elite politics and "yellow" (liberal) forces
- Duterte tapped into desire for "discipline" and stability [Source: [Democratic Erosion](https://democratic-erosion.org/2025/02/14/democratic-backsliding-in-the-philippines/)]

### C. Duterte's Davao Record

Rodrigo Duterte served as mayor of Davao City for over 22 years (seven terms), transforming the "murder capital" into what he claimed was one of the safest cities in the world. [Source: [PBS Frontline](https://www.pbs.org/wgbh/frontline/article/bloody-drug-war-rodrigo-duterte-iron-mayor/)]

**The Davao Death Squad:**
- Vigilante group operating under Duterte's mayoralty since the 1990s
- Estimated 1,020-1,424 killings between 1998 and 2015
- Victims included street children, petty criminals, and drug users
- Youngest victim: 12-year-old boy [Source: [Human Rights Watch](https://www.hrw.org/news/2015/07/17/rodrigo-duterte-rise-philippines-death-squad-mayor)]

**Duterte's Admissions:**
- In October 2024, Duterte confirmed to the Philippine Senate: "I had a death squad of seven"
- Former police officer Arthur Lascanas (2017) confessed to leading the squad
- Duterte admitted shooting three people while mayor
- 2007: UN Special Rapporteur Philip Alston stated: "A death squad operates in Davao City, with men routinely killing street children and others in broad daylight" [Source: [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/10/28/philippines-ex-president-duterte-says-he-kept-a-death-squad-as-mayor)]

**DDS Structure (per Senator Leila de Lima):**
- Two distinct periods: 1988-1998 and 2001-2016
- Later transformed into official Heinous Crimes Investigation Section
- Not a loosely connected group but a "highly structured and state-backed death squad" [Source: [Philippine News Agency](https://www.pna.gov.ph/articles/1236090)]

## II. Rise to Power: The 2016 Election

### A. Campaign Strategy

Duterte positioned himself as a political outsider from Mindanao, a region often neglected by Manila-based elites. [Source: [Wikipedia - Rodrigo Duterte 2016 presidential campaign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rodrigo_Duterte_2016_presidential_campaign)]

**Anti-Establishment Appeal:**
- "One Voice, One Nation" slogan referenced Manila outsider status
- Positioned himself against current political system, not just ruling party
- Called himself "socialist" and opponent of corruption
- Rejected statesman rhetoric for "tabloid language" [Source: [CIRSD](https://www.cirsd.org/en/horizons/horizons-winter-2020-issue-no-15/is-duterte-a-populist-rhetoric-vs-reality)]

**Law-and-Order Rhetoric:**
- Promised to eliminate illegal drugs within "three to six months"
- Campaign urged people to "kill drug addicts"
- Scorched earth approach immediately set him apart
- Voters elected him knowing he would implement illiberal policies [Source: [Journal of Democracy](https://www.journalofdemocracy.org/articles/the-vote-in-the-philippines-electing-a-strongman/)]

**Unconventional Style:**
- Flashed middle finger to press without consequence
- Cursed the Pope with no negative impact
- Used Bisaya language to build ethnic appeal among co-ethnics
- Measured political incorrectness enhanced credibility [Source: [ECPS](https://www.populismstudies.org/rodrigo-roa-duterte-a-jingoist-misogynist-penal-populist/)]

## III. The Drug War (2016-2022)

### A. Oplan Tokhang and Operation Double Barrel

Immediately upon taking office, Duterte launched a nationwide anti-drug campaign modeled on his Davao approach. [Source: [Wikipedia - Philippine drug war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippine_drug_war)]

**Operation Components:**
- *Oplan Tokhang* (from Cebuano: tuktok/knock + hangyo/plead): Police visit suspected drug users/dealers to persuade surrender
- *Oplan HVT*: Operations targeting high-value drug syndicates
- "Davao Model" replicated nationwide at Duterte's request [Source: [HRW](https://www.hrw.org/report/2017/03/02/license-kill/philippine-police-killings-dutertes-war-drugs)]

**Duterte's Public Statements:**
- "My instruction to the officers was, 'encourage the criminals to fight... and when they fight, kill them'"
- Repeatedly told police to "shoot to kill" when enforcing drug laws
- When told 32 people died in one night: "That's beautiful" [Source: [Rappler](https://www.rappler.com/features/newsbreak/in-depth/143905-war-on-drugs-pnp-duterte/)]

### B. Death Toll Estimates

**Official Government Figures:**
- Philippine National Police: 6,252 drug suspects killed (2016-2022)
- Police claimed all killings were "nanlaban" (they fought back) [Source: [Wikipedia - Philippine drug war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippine_drug_war)]

**Independent Estimates:**
- Human Rights Watch: 12,000+ by January 2018
- UN High Commissioner: 8,000+ by June 2020
- ICC Prosecutor estimate: 12,000-30,000 (July 2016 - March 2019)
- Domestic human rights groups: 2-3x higher than UN figures [Source: [HRW](https://www.hrw.org/news/2018/01/18/philippines-dutertes-drug-war-claims-12000-lives)]

**Victims:**
- Predominantly urban poor
- Youngest victim: 5 months old
- At least 73 children killed
- Average: 6 "drug personalities" killed per day (July 2016 - May 2019) [Source: [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2020/06/1067462)]

### C. Methods and Patterns

**Police Operations:**
- Extrajudicial killings in faked "buy-bust" encounters
- Police planted guns, spent ammunition, and drug packets on victims
- False claims of self-defense ("nanlaban") [Source: [HRW](https://www.hrw.org/news/2017/03/02/philippines-police-deceit-drug-war-killings)]

**Vigilante Killings:**
- "Riding-in-tandem" attacks: gunmen on motorcycles
- Victims found with heads wrapped in duct tape
- Cardboard signs: "I'm a drug pusher, don't be like me" ("drug pusher huwag tularan")
- Some vigilantes admitted being hired by police
- Senior police officer confirmed 10 "special ops" death squads operating [Source: [Salon](https://www.salon.com/2025/03/22/dutertes-bloody-was-really-a-on-the-poor/)]

**Suspension and Resumption:**
- Tokhang suspended January 2017 after police abducted South Korean businessman
- PDEA took over as lead agency October 2017
- Police resumed operations January 2018 in "supporting role" [Source: [The Diplomat](https://thediplomat.com/2018/02/dutertes-drug-war-in-the-philippines-new-campaign-old-problems/)]

## IV. Media Suppression

### A. ABS-CBN Shutdown (2020)

ABS-CBN, the Philippines' largest broadcast network, was shut down during the COVID-19 pandemic after its 25-year franchise expired. [Source: [Wikipedia - ABS-CBN franchise renewal controversy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ABS-CBN_franchise_renewal_controversy)]

**Timeline:**
- May 4, 2020: Congressional franchise expired
- May 5, 2020: NTC issued cease-and-desist order
- July 10, 2020: House committee voted 70-11 to deny franchise renewal [Source: [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/7/10/dutertes-congress-allies-back-order-to-shut-philippines-abs-cbn)]

**Duterte's Role:**
- Long harbored enmity toward ABS-CBN for refusing to air his 2016 campaign ads
- Called franchise renewal bid "piece of garbage"
- Malaca√±ang initially claimed "neutral stance"
- Three days before leaving office, Duterte admitted using "presidential powers" against ABS-CBN [Source: [VERA Files](https://verafiles.org/articles/vera-files-fact-check-duterte-admits-using-presidential-powers-vs-abs-cbn-franchise-renewal-contradicting-previous-neutral-stance)]

**Impact:**
- First time ABS-CBN off air since 1986 revolution (was seized under Marcos martial law)
- 4,552 jobs cut (nearly half of workforce)
- Losses surged 400% to P13.5 billion
- 69 million Filipinos lost access to ABS-CBN News [Source: [Inquirer](https://business.inquirer.net/324444/abs-cbn-loss-hits-p13-5b-after-duterte-shutdown-campaign-amid-pandemic-recession)]

### B. Rappler and Maria Ressa

Maria Ressa, CEO of digital news site Rappler, faced sustained legal persecution for critical coverage of the administration. [Source: [Wikipedia - Maria Ressa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maria_Ressa)]

**Legal Cases:**
- 23 total legal cases filed against Ressa, Rappler, and colleagues since 2017
- 5 tax evasion charges (all acquitted by 2023)
- Cyber libel conviction (June 2020): up to 6 years prison
- 10 arrest warrants issued 2019-2021
- Anti-dummy case (acquitted June 2025) [Source: [PBS Frontline](https://www.pbs.org/wgbh/frontline/article/rappler-maria-ressa-sec-orders-shutdown-cyber-libel-conviction/)]

**Cyber Libel Case:**
- Based on 2012 article (law passed after publication)
- Conviction based on technicality that fixing a typo constituted "republishing"
- Called "frontal assault on freedom of the press" [Source: [Amnesty International](https://www.amnesty.org.ph/2023/01/nobel-laureate-maria-ressa-acquitted-on-politically-motivated-tax-evasion-charges/)]

**Recognition:**
- 2021 Nobel Peace Prize (with Dmitry Muratov)
- Cited for "using freedom of expression to expose abuse of power, use of violence, and growing authoritarianism" [Source: [Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/biography/Maria-Ressa)]

### C. Journalist Killings

**Statistics:**
- 23 journalists killed under Duterte (2016-2022)
- 32 incidents of red-tagging of journalists
- 16 journalists killed by May 2020 [Source: [HRW](https://www.hrw.org/news/2021/12/10/killing-journalist-criminal-libel-philippines)]

## V. Martial Law in Mindanao (2017-2019)

### A. The Marawi Siege

On May 23, 2017, ISIS-affiliated militants from the Maute group and Abu Sayyaf seized parts of Marawi City in Mindanao. [Source: [Wikipedia - Siege of Marawi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Marawi)]

**The Battle:**
- Militants seized Marawi City Hall, Mindanao State University, hospital, city jail
- Set fire to Saint Mary's Cathedral and educational institutions
- Objective: Declare wilayat (Islamic State province) in Lanao del Sur
- Took priest and churchgoers hostage [Source: [NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/storyline/isis-terror/why-duterte-declared-martial-law-southern-philippines-over-isis-linked-n764546)]

**Casualties and Displacement:**
- 5-month battle (ended October 17, 2017)
- 1,100+ people killed
- 500,000+ displaced
- October 17: Duterte declared Marawi "liberated" after deaths of leaders Omar Maute and Isnilon Hapilon [Source: [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2019/12/30/what-did-dutertes-martial-law-achieve-in-mindanao)]

### B. Martial Law Extensions

**Proclamation No. 216:**
- Issued May 23, 2017: Martial law and suspension of habeas corpus in Mindanao
- Extended three times by Duterte-allied Congress:
  - First extension: Through December 2017
  - Second extension: Through December 2018
  - Third extension: Through December 31, 2019
- Total duration: 31 months [Source: [Wikipedia - Proclamation No. 216](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proclamation_No._216)]

**Criticism:**
- 130 extrajudicial killings in first year of martial law (per Mindanao human rights group)
- Most victims: peasants and indigenous Lumads
- Jolo church bombing (January 2019): 20+ killed despite martial law
- Duterte's claim of "no abuses" rated FALSE by fact-checkers [Source: [VERA Files](https://verafiles.org/articles/vera-files-fact-check-duterte-claim-no-abuses-mindanao-marti)]

### C. Marawi Reconstruction Failure

- By November 2019: More than P4 billion of P10 billion rehabilitation fund unreleased
- Two years after "liberation": Marawi remained a ghost town
- Reconstruction plagued by bureaucratic delays [Source: [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2019/7/22/a-failure-marawi-verdict-on-duterte-ahead-of-annual-address)]

## VI. China Pivot and Foreign Policy

### A. Shift Toward Beijing

Upon securing the presidency, Duterte actively courted China and "set aside" the Philippines' 2016 arbitration victory in the South China Sea. [Source: [The Diplomat](https://thediplomat.com/2024/09/parsing-the-philippines-pivot-to-china-under-rodrigo-duterte/)]

**October 2016 Beijing Visit:**
- First Filipino president to choose Beijing as first major foreign trip
- China offered $24 billion in investments and BRI infrastructure projects
- Duterte announced "separation from the United States"
- Broke historic alliance pattern (previous presidents visited Washington or Tokyo first) [Source: [Asia Times](https://asiatimes.com/2022/04/how-duterte-fell-for-chinas-bait-and-switch/)]

**Build, Build, Build Program:**
- $160 billion infrastructure undertaking
- Intended to leverage BRI resources
- Projects included Mindanao railway system [Source: [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s44282-024-00076-2)]

### B. Failed Outcomes

**Infrastructure Disappointment:**
- Of $24 billion promised in 2016, only three projects completed by 2022:
  - One dam
  - One irrigation project
  - One bridge
- Mega-railway project in Mindanao never materialized [Source: [Asia Times](https://asiatimes.com/2022/04/how-duterte-fell-for-chinas-bait-and-switch/)]

**Continued South China Sea Tensions:**
- Chinese coast guard continued seizing Filipino fishermen's catches
- China expanded militarization of artificial islands
- Military facilities built on Subi Reef, Fiery Cross, Mischief Reef
- Chinese loan terms worse than those offered by Japan [Source: [FPRI](https://www.fpri.org/article/2018/09/second-thoughts-duterte-and-the-south-china-sea/)]

### C. US Alliance Restoration

**Late-Term Reversal:**
- July 2021: Duterte restored Visiting Forces Agreement after threatened withdrawal
- US Defense Secretary Lloyd Austin visit
- Signs of US-Philippines realignment in final year
- Successor Marcos Jr. continued BRI projects but canceled others due to tensions [Source: [Lowy Institute](https://www.lowyinstitute.org/the-interpreter/reset-us-philippines-relations-tells-yet-another-china-fumble)]

## VII. Opposition Suppression

### A. Senator Leila de Lima

Senator Leila de Lima, chair of the Senate Justice Committee who had investigated Duterte's death squads, was arrested in February 2017. [Source: [Wikipedia - Leila de Lima](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leila_de_Lima)]

**Background:**
- As Justice Secretary (2010-2015), investigated Davao Death Squad
- As Senator, conducted hearings on drug war killings
- Duterte directed her persecution in response to investigations [Source: [HRW](https://www.hrw.org/news/2023/05/15/latest-de-lima-acquittal-exposes-philippine-justice-systems-politicization)]

**Detention:**
- Arrested February 2017 on drug trafficking charges
- Held for 6 years and 9 months
- November 13, 2023: Released on bail after paying P300,000 bond
- June 24, 2024: Fully acquitted on all charges [Source: [NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/news/world/ex-philippine-senator-fought-dutertes-harsh-drug-crackdown-freed-bail-rcna125059)]

**International Response:**
- EU Parliament demanded release
- US lawmakers called for freedom
- UN Human Rights experts: charges "likely politically motivated"
- EU Ambassador: Release "a significant step for the rule of law" [Source: [UN OHCHR](https://www.ohchr.org/en/press-releases/2023/11/philippines-un-experts-welcome-release-former-senator-leila-de-lima-bail)]

### B. Red-Tagging

"Red-tagging" - publicly accusing individuals of communist insurgency ties - became a deadly practice under Duterte. [Source: [HRW](https://www.hrw.org/news/2022/01/17/philippines-end-deadly-red-tagging-activists)]

**Mechanisms:**
- Government social media posts, press conferences, and posters
- 2018: Duterte created National Task Force to End Local Communist Armed Conflict
- 2020: Anti-Terrorism Act with broad language enabled bypassing due process
- Qualifying Communist Party as terrorist group [Source: [Human Rights Foundation](https://hrf.org/latest/red-tagging-in-the-philippines-a-license-to-kill/)]

**Statistics:**
- 427 incidents of killings documented (July 2016 - December 2021)
- 537 frustrated killings
- 1,161 activists arrested and detained
- 801 political prisoners by end of Duterte term
- 126 extrajudicial killings of Indigenous leaders (2016-2021) [Source: [France 24](https://www.france24.com/en/tv-shows/focus/20230110-political-repression-in-the-philippines-the-deadly-red-tagging-of-activists)]

## VIII. Human Rights Documentation

### A. UN Human Rights Council Report (2020)

In June 2020, UN High Commissioner Michelle Bachelet released a comprehensive report on the Philippines. [Source: [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2020/06/1067462)]

**Key Findings:**
- "Drug campaign-related killings appear to have a widespread and systematic character"
- Possibly more than 25,000 killed (July 2016 - January 2020)
- "Carried out without due regard for the rule of law, due process, and human rights"
- 73 children linked to drug campaign deaths; youngest victim 5 months old [Source: [Rappler](https://www.rappler.com/newsbreak/data-documents/262853-full-report-united-nations-human-rights-report-philippines-extrajudicial-drug-war-killings/)]

**Recommendations:**
- Called for international accountability measures
- State obligation to conduct independent investigations
- Council should consider options if domestic mechanisms fail [Source: [The Diplomat](https://thediplomat.com/2020/07/un-rights-office-criticizes-impunity-and-systematic-violence-of-philippines-drug-war/)]

### B. Human Rights Watch and Amnesty International

**HRW "License to Kill" Report (2017):**
- Documented Philippine National Police carrying out extrajudicial killings
- Police falsely claimed self-defense
- Evidence planting of guns, ammunition, and drugs [Source: [HRW](https://www.hrw.org/report/2017/03/02/license-kill/philippine-police-killings-dutertes-war-drugs)]

**Amnesty International "My Job is to Kill" (2020):**
- Documented climate of near-absolute impunity
- Normalization of extrajudicial executions
- At least 6,600 killed by police (July 2016 - May 2019) [Source: [Amnesty International](https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/press-release/2020/09/philippines-un-pressure-end-killings/)]

**UN Documentation (2015-2019):**
- 248 human rights defenders, legal professionals, journalists, and trade unionists killed
- 76 land and environmental rights defenders killed (July 2016 - November 2018)
- Only 4 convictions out of thousands of drug war cases [Source: [FIDH](https://www.fidh.org/en/issues/human-rights-defenders/philippines-casualties-on-the-rise-in-president-duterte-s-war-on)]

## IX. ICC Investigation and Arrest

### A. Investigation Timeline

**Preliminary Examination:**
- February 2018: ICC announced preliminary examination
- March 2018: Duterte ordered Philippines withdrawal from Rome Statute
- March 2019: Withdrawal took effect [Source: [ICC](https://www.icc-cpi.int/philippines)]

**Investigation Authorization:**
- September 15, 2021: Pre-Trial Chamber I authorized investigation
- Scope: Crimes committed November 1, 2011 - March 16, 2019 (while Philippines was State Party)
- Late 2021: Duterte administration sought to suspend investigation, claiming domestic review [Source: [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2021/9/15/philippines-icc-agrees-to-probe-dutertes-war-on-drugs)]

**Investigation Resumption:**
- January 26, 2023: Pre-Trial Chamber granted prosecutor's request to resume
- Court rejected Philippines' arguments about domestic proceedings [Source: [HRW](https://www.hrw.org/news/2023/02/13/international-criminal-courts-philippines-investigation)]

### B. Arrest and Detention

**Arrest Warrant:**
- February 10, 2025: Prosecutor applied for arrest warrant
- March 7, 2025: Pre-Trial Chamber I issued warrant (initially sealed)
- March 11, 2025: Warrant made public; Duterte arrested at Ninoy Aquino International Airport
- March 12, 2025: Surrendered to ICC custody [Source: [HRW](https://www.hrw.org/news/2025/03/12/philippines-duterte-arrested-icc-warrant)]

**Charges:**
- Crimes against humanity: murder, torture, rape
- 76 specific murders cited in indictment (made public September 22, 2025)
- 57 during presidency, 19 as Davao mayor [Source: [PBS](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/international-criminal-court-details-crimes-against-humanity-charges-against-former-philippine-president-duterte)]

**Legal Proceedings:**
- March 14, 2025: Initial appearance via videolink
- September 8, 2025: Confirmation hearing postponed for fitness evaluation
- November 28, 2025: Appeals judges rejected release on health grounds
- Defense led by British-Israeli lawyer Nicholas Kaufman [Source: [PBS](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/icc-judges-reject-request-to-release-ex-philippine-president-duterte-from-custody)]

**Historical Significance:**
- First Asian former head of state arrested and surrendered to ICC
- First Southeast Asian leader arrested by ICC [Source: [Asia Media Centre](https://www.asiamediacentre.org.nz/former-philippine-president-arrested-by-icc-for-deadly-war-on-drugs)]

## X. Legacy and Political Dynasty

### A. The UniTeam Alliance (2022)

Duterte's legacy was intended to continue through his daughter Sara and an alliance with the Marcos family. [Source: [Wikipedia - UniTeam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UniTeam)]

**Formation:**
- November 2021: UniTeam formed to support Ferdinand "Bongbong" Marcos Jr. and Sara Duterte
- Sara withdrew from presidential race to become Marcos's running mate
- Combined regional bases: Marcos (northern Luzon) + Duterte (Mindanao) [Source: [Sun.Star](https://www.sunstar.com.ph/davao/revisiting-uniteam-sara-duterte-bongbong-marcos-forge-alliance-amid-2022-election-epoch)]

**2022 Election Victory:**
- Sara Duterte: 32,208,417 votes (margin of 22 million)
- First VP elected by majority since 1986
- Most votes for any single-winner election in Philippine history
- Sara appointed Secretary of Education [Source: [Wikipedia - Sara Duterte](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sara_Duterte)]

### B. Alliance Collapse (2024-2025)

The Marcos-Duterte alliance rapidly deteriorated. [Source: [Inquirer](https://newsinfo.inquirer.net/2158424/2025-sees-relationship-between-marcos-sara-duterte-break-beyond-repair)]

**Breakdown:**
- June 2024: Sara resigned as Education Secretary
- September 2024: Sara stated she and Marcos were "never friends"
- Sara revealed alleged assassination plot against Marcos, First Lady, and Speaker Romualdez
- P612.5 million confidential funds scandal emerged [Source: [FULCRUM](https://fulcrum.sg/unravelling-of-the-marcos-duterte-dynastic-alliance/)]

**Sara Duterte Impeachment:**
- February 5, 2025: 215 House members signed impeachment complaint
- Charges: corruption, assassination plotting, involvement in extrajudicial killings, incitement to insurrection
- July 2025: Supreme Court struck down complaint as unconstitutional [Source: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/02/05/asia/philippines-vp-duterte-impeachment-intl-hnk)]

### C. Current Status

**The Dutertes in Crisis:**
- Rodrigo Duterte: In ICC custody awaiting trial
- Sara Duterte: Remains VP; frontrunner for 2028 presidential election despite proceedings
- Duterte-backed candidates won Senate seats in 2025 midterms
- China policy remains contentious [Source: [Chatham House](https://www.chathamhouse.org/2025/11/marcos-duterte-feud-undermining-philippine-security-south-china-sea)]

## XI. Scholarly Debates

### A. Penal Populism

Nicole Curato's foundational research (2016) describes Duterte's appeal as "penal populism" built on two reinforcing logics. [Source: [SAGE Journals](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/186810341603500305)]

**Key Arguments:**
- *Politics of Anxiety*: Language of crisis, danger, and uncertainty about crime
- *Politics of Hope*: Reclaiming democratic agency through strong leadership
- Belief that "deviant activity" deprives ordinary people of safety
- "Elite-driven" legal system doesn't address common people's concerns [Source: [Cambridge Core](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/journal-of-east-asian-studies/article/new-penal-populism-rodrigo-duterte-public-opinion-and-the-war-on-drugs-in-the-philippines/4AE0C4975F0D73EC0631991A3DE646D6)]

**Paradox:**
- Voters attached to democracy can still favor illiberal policies in law-and-order domains
- Democratic regression may result from voting preferences of pro-democratic voters
- Crime was highly salient issue enabling penal populist appeal [Source: [Cambridge Core](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/journal-of-east-asian-studies/article/new-penal-populism-rodrigo-duterte-public-opinion-and-the-war-on-drugs-in-the-philippines/4AE0C4975F0D73EC0631991A3DE646D6)]

### B. Democratic Backsliding

Scholars debate whether Filipino voters have become illiberal or remain contingent supporters of illiberal policies. [Source: [SAGE Journals](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/20578911221136263)]

**The "Illiberal Voter" Thesis:**
- Some scholars argued Filipinos acquired "taste for illiberal rule"
- Kasuya and Calimbahin (2024): Survey data does not support this claim
- Filipino voters are "contingent supporters" while maintaining democratic baseline [Source: [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/365931099_Democratic_backsliding_in_the_Philippines_Are_voters_becoming_illiberal)]

**Duterte as Unprecedented:**
- First president elected knowing he would implement illiberal policies
- Maintained high approval despite attacks on democratic institutions
- List experiments suggest popularity may have been inflated by social desirability bias [Source: [Cambridge Core](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/journal-of-east-asian-studies/article/abs/pretending-to-support-dutertes-popularity-and-democratic-backsliding-in-the-philippines/DA8DB740FAF9AD325491853DD5CCDF53)]

### C. Authoritarian Populism

Mark Thompson's research characterizes Duterte's rule as "illiberal democracy" comparable to Orban's Hungary. [Source: [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/334450487_Thompson_Mark_2019_The_Rise_of_Illiberal_Democracy_in_the_Philippines)]

**Key Features:**
- "Right populism" with violent drug war as centerpiece
- Consistent critique of liberal democratic theory and practice
- Human rights portrayed as obstacle to development
- Emphasis on Filipino, Asian, and developing country contexts [Source: [ECPS](https://www.populismstudies.org/the-philippines-from-people-power-to-democratic-backsliding/)]

**Comparative Analysis (Nair, 2023):**
- Duterte compared to Sukarno (Indonesia) and Sihanouk (Cambodia)
- Shared strong distrust of institutions
- Discredited parties, attacked individuals, undercut checks and balances
- All embodied "populism as discursive and stylistic repertoire" [Source: [SAGE Journals](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/13540661231173866)]

### D. Social Media and Populism

Scholars note Duterte's pioneering use of social media in Southeast Asian politics. [Source: [Intellect](https://intellectdiscover.com/content/journals/10.1386/macp_00097_1)]

**Mechanisms:**
- Social media facilitated populist appeal and legitimacy
- Tool for forming popular beliefs, sentiments, and opinions
- Enabled governance by conspiracy narratives
- Bypassed traditional media gatekeepers [Source: [Yale Review of International Studies](https://yris.yira.org/column/dictatorship-of-the-vengeful-the-rise-of-civic-authoritarianism-in-rodrigo-dutertes-philippines/)]
