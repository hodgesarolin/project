## Overview

Marshal Ion Victor Antonescu (15 June 1882 - 1 June 1946) was a Romanian military officer who presided over two successive wartime dictatorships as Prime Minister and Conducator ("Leader") during most of [[World War II]]. His regime made Romania [[Nazi Germany]]'s most significant military ally on the [[Eastern Front]], contributing the third-largest Axis army in Europe. The Antonescu regime was directly responsible for the deaths of an estimated 280,000 to 400,000 Jews and over 11,000 Roma, making Romania the largest perpetrator of the [[Holocaust]] after Germany itself.

> [!important] Key Distinction
> Unlike most other Holocaust perpetrator nations, Romania carried out its genocidal campaign independently, without direct intervention from the German SS. The Antonescu regime initiated and organized its own extermination program, particularly in [[Transnistria]] and the recovered territories of [[Bessarabia]] and [[Bukovina]].

## 1. Early Life and Military Career

### Background and Education

Ion Antonescu was born on 15 June 1882 in Pitesti, northwest of Bucharest, into an upper-middle class Romanian Orthodox family with military tradition. His father, an army officer, sent him to attend the Infantry and Cavalry School in Craiova.

**Key biographical details:**
- Graduated from Cavalry School as top of class in 1904
- Military academy graduation and commissioning: 1911
- Close relationship with his mother, Lita Baranga
- Parents' divorce (father married a Jewish convert to Orthodoxy) was reportedly traumatic

### Early Military Distinctions

Antonescu built his military reputation through several key engagements:

| Event | Year | Role |
|-------|------|------|
| [[1907 Romanian Peasants' Revolt]] | 1907 | Repression as lieutenant in Galati region |
| [[Second Balkan War]] | 1913 | Won Romania's highest military decoration |
| [[World War I]] | 1916-1918 | Chief of Staff for Marshal [[Constantin Prezan]] |
| [[Battle of Marasesti]] | 1917 | Key tactical contributor to Romanian defense |

> [!quote] Military Reputation
> "Antonescu was considered the primary reason for Romania's successful defense against the attempted invasion of Moldavia by Field Marshal Mackensen in the second half of 1917."

### Interwar Career

**Key positions:**
- **1922-1926**: Military attache in France and Great Britain
- **1927-1930**: Commander of the Scoala Superioara de Razboi (Upper School of War)
- **1933-1934**: Chief of the Great Headquarters of the Army
- **1937-1938**: Defense Minister under [[King Carol II]]

His far-right sympathies and connections to the [[Iron Guard]] led to his dismissal and detainment by King Carol II in the late 1930s.

## 2. Rise to Power (1940)

### The Crisis of 1940

Romania suffered catastrophic territorial losses that summer, losing approximately one-third of its 1939 territory and population:

**Territorial Losses:**

| Territory | Lost To | Date | Size |
|-----------|---------|------|------|
| [[Bessarabia]] and [[Northern Bukovina]] | [[Soviet Union]] | June 1940 | Soviet ultimatum |
| [[Northern Transylvania]] | [[Hungary]] | 30 August 1940 | [[Second Vienna Award]] |
| [[Southern Dobrogea]] | [[Bulgaria]] | September 1940 | [[Treaty of Craiova]] |

The [[Second Vienna Award]] forced Romania to cede 43,492 km2 with 2.67 million people to Hungary, arbitrated by Nazi Germany and Fascist Italy.

### Antonescu Takes Power

The territorial disasters provoked national outrage directed at [[King Carol II]]:

**Timeline:**
- **4 September 1940**: Antonescu appointed Prime Minister with absolute powers
- **6 September 1940**: Antonescu forced King Carol II to abdicate
- **6 September 1940**: 19-year-old [[King Michael I]] ascended to throne
- **6 September 1940**: King Michael granted Antonescu unlimited power as Conducator

> [!note] German Influence
> When Carol proved reluctant to make Antonescu Prime Minister, German Minister Fabricius was asked to phone Carol to tell him that the Reich wanted Antonescu as Prime Minister - which he promptly did.

## 3. The National Legionary State (September 1940 - February 1941)

### Formation

On **14 September 1940**, Romania was proclaimed a [[National Legionary State]] by royal decree - a totalitarian regime representing an uneasy partnership between Antonescu and [[Horia Sima]]'s [[Iron Guard]] (also known as the Legion of the Archangel Michael).

**Power-sharing arrangement:**
- Romania declared a "National Legionary State"
- [[Iron Guard]] became the country's only legal party
- Antonescu became the Legion's honorary leader
- [[Horia Sima]] became Deputy Premier
- Four other Legionnaires joined the cabinet

### Iron Guard Excesses

Once in power, the Iron Guard escalated violence:

**Legionnaire Crimes (September 1940 - January 1941):**
- Ratcheted up antisemitic legislation
- Pursued campaign of pogroms with impunity
- Conducted political assassinations
- **27 November 1940**: [[Jilava Prison Massacre]] - more than 60 former dignitaries executed
- Murdered historian [[Nicolae Iorga]] and economist [[Virgil Madgearu]]

> [!warning] Arms Stockpile
> By early 1941, the Legionnaires in Bucharest alone possessed 5,000 guns (rifles, revolvers, and machine guns) plus numerous hand grenades.

### Tensions Between Partners

Antonescu became increasingly frustrated with the Iron Guard:

- Guard seizures of Jewish property benefited only Legionnaires, not the state
- Disorderly conduct threatened economic stability
- Antonescu demanded "order and legality"
- Germans feared Legionnaires were unprepared to administer the country effectively

---

## 4. Break with the Iron Guard (January 1941)

### Securing Hitler's Approval

On **14 January 1941**, Antonescu met with [[Adolf Hitler]] at a German-Romanian summit:

**Key outcomes:**
- Hitler revealed plans for [[Operation Barbarossa]] - the invasion of the Soviet Union
- Hitler asked Romania to participate
- Antonescu gained Hitler's tacit agreement to eliminate the Iron Guard

### The Legionnaires' Rebellion (21-23 January 1941)

Antonescu moved to neutralize the Guard on **19 January 1941**:
- Cancelled "Romanianization commissar" positions (lucrative jobs held by Legionnaires)
- Fired Interior Minister [[Constantin Petrovicescu]]
- Replaced Security Police and Bucharest police commanders with loyal military men

**The Trigger:**
On **20 January 1941**, a German officer (Major Helmuth Doring) was killed in Bucharest by a Greek citizen working for Allied intelligence. Though unrelated to the Guard, this murder sparked the rebellion.

**The Rebellion:**
- Armed Legionnaires captured the Interior Ministry, police stations, and government buildings
- Opened fire on soldiers attempting to retake buildings
- Seized control of media to prevent Antonescu's addresses from being broadcast

### The Bucharest Pogrom

Simultaneously with their political uprising, the Legionnaires launched a violent pogrom:

**Atrocities committed:**
- **125 Jews murdered** in Bucharest
- Jews seized from synagogues and streets, some from prepared lists
- Synagogues ignited using fuel tankers; Legionnaires "danced around the flames"
- Kahal Grande (main Sephardic synagogue) burned completely
- 1,274 businesses, shops, and homes destroyed

> [!quote] Yad Vashem Documentation
> "The Legionnaires ignited the Jewish synagogues and danced around the flames, roaring with joy."

### Suppression and Aftermath

Antonescu crushed the rebellion within four days:

**Casualties:**
- 125 Jews killed
- 30 soldiers died confronting rebels
- 200 trucks of looted property recovered (not including money and jewelry)

**Consequences:**
- Iron Guard banned
- 9,000 Legionnaires arrested
- 1,842 sentenced to various terms (months to life imprisonment)
- [[Horia Sima]] and leaders fled to Nazi Germany
- **14 February 1941**: National Legionary State formally abolished
- Romania declared a "National and Social State"

## 5. Alliance with Nazi Germany

### Joining the Axis

Romania formally aligned with the Axis powers through a series of steps:

**Key dates:**
- **23 November 1940**: Romania signed the [[Tripartite Pact]]
- Romania was the third country to join after Hungary (20 November) and before Slovakia (24 November)

**Strategic motivations:**
- Protection against further Soviet incursions
- Hope of recovering Northern Transylvania from Hungary
- Romania supplied over 60% of German oil imports by late 1940

### The Hitler-Antonescu Relationship

Antonescu met with Hitler at least **10 times** between fall 1940 and summer 1944, mainly in Austria and East Prussia.

**Nature of the relationship:**
- Hitler respected Antonescu for his military qualities and organizational efficiency
- Antonescu was awarded the [[Knight's Cross]] by Hitler
- Romania maintained sovereignty throughout the war - never occupied by Wehrmacht
- The relationship was characterized by mutual respect unusual among Axis partners

> [!quote] Historian Assessment
> "Romania was unique in many important respects. The country was one of the few to maintain its sovereignty throughout the Second World War, in large measure because of the Fuhrer's respect for Antonescu."

### Ideological Alignment

On **12 June 1941**, Hitler revealed to Antonescu the "special" nature of [[Operation Barbarossa]]:
- A war to "annihilate" the forces of "[[Judeo-Bolshevism]]"
- A "war of extermination" to be fought without mercy
- Antonescu "completely accepted Hitler's ideas about Operation Barbarossa as a 'race war'"

> [!quote] Foreign Policy Analysis
> "Romania's holy war complemented Nazi Germany's race war and bound the two tightly together on the Eastern Front even after Operation Barbarossa failed to destroy the Soviet Union."

## 6. The Holocaust in Romania

### Overview

The Antonescu regime was directly responsible for one of the largest genocides of World War II outside Germany:

**Key statistics:**
- **280,000-400,000 Jews killed** in Romanian-controlled territories
- **11,000-36,000 Roma killed**
- Romania ranks **first among Holocaust perpetrator countries other than Germany**

> [!important] Independent Action
> "Romania being the only ally of the Third Reich that carried out a genocidal campaign without the intervention of Heinrich Himmler's SS."

### Antisemitic Legislation and "Romanianization"

**Policy framework:**
- "Romanianization" - policy of excluding Jews and Roma from economic life
- Property and business seizures
- Exclusion from employment and professions

**Key measures:**
- **September 1940**: Proclamation of National Legionary State triggered Romanianization laws
- **28 March 1941**: Jewish buildings in cities nationalized
- **1941**: All Jews aged 20-50 conscripted into forced labor
- Jews taxed for those unable to work due to illness
- Deportations decreed as punishment for labor regulation violations

**Forced labor statistics:**
- Over **105,000 Jews** forced to work in camps, battalions, and institutions (1941-1944)
- Extraordinary physical and psychological suffering
- Hunger, inadequate shelter, dangerous conditions

### The Iasi Pogrom (28-30 June 1941)

The [[Iasi Pogrom]] was one of the deadliest massacres of Jews during World War II, occurring just days after Romania joined Operation Barbarossa.

**Background:**
- **24 June 1941**: Soviet air raid on Iasi caused minimal damage
- Hysteria erupted; rumors spread that Jews lit beacons to guide Soviet bombers
- Posters appeared encouraging violence against Jews

**The massacre (28-30 June):**
- Romanian and German soldiers, SSI (intelligence service), police, and civilians participated
- Thousands murdered in homes and streets
- Thousands arrested and taken to police headquarters
- Next day: soldiers shot thousands of Jews held in police headquarters yard

**Perpetrators:**
- SSI (Special Information Service)
- German Abwehr
- Neighbors, antisemitic movement supporters
- Students, railway workers, veterans, civil servants

**Death toll: 13,000-13,266** (one-third of Iasi's Jewish population)

### The Death Trains

Following the initial massacre, approximately 4,300 survivors were loaded onto sealed boxcars:

**First Death Train:**
- Departed early morning, 30 June 1941
- Decorated with slogans: "Communist Jews" and "Killers of German and Romanian soldiers"
- 17-hour circuitous journey to various stations
- Reached final destination on 6 July
- **Only 1,076 of original passengers survived**

**Second Death Train:**
- Departed 30 June 1941
- 1,902 Jews packed into 18 railcars (plus 80 corpses)
- 8-hour journey to Podul Iloaei (only 20 km from Iasi)
- **Only 708 survived**

**Combined death toll on trains: approximately 2,600** (died of thirst and suffocation)

> [!note] Documentation
> "This pogrom is one of the most thoroughly visually documented events of the Holocaust in that members of the Romanian intelligence services photographed the continuing massacre that they themselves were coordinating."

### The Odessa Massacre (October 1941)

Following the Romanian capture of [[Odessa]] after a two-month siege (16 October 1941), one of the largest massacres of the Holocaust occurred.

**The Trigger:**
- **22 October 1941**: Radio-controlled mine (planted by Red Army before surrender) exploded
- Romanian military headquarters destroyed
- 67 killed, including Romanian General [[Ioan Glogojeanu]]
- Jews and Communists blamed

**The massacre (22-24 October):**
- Romanian soldiers, [[Einsatzgruppe]] SS, and local ethnic Germans participated
- **25,000-34,000 Jews murdered** in initial massacre
- Methods: shooting and burning

**Broader death toll in Odessa region:**
- **115,000-180,000 Jews** killed in Odessa and surrounding districts of Holta and Berezivka
- **130,000 Jews** killed in Odessa region from 1941-1945

### Transnistria: The Romanian Death Zone

[[Transnistria Governorate]] - the territory between the Dniester and Bug rivers - became the primary killing field for Romanian Jews.

**Deportations:**
- **15 September 1941**: Deportations began
- Target population: Jews of Bessarabia, Bukovina, and northern Moldavia declared "enemy population"
- **Mid-November 1941**: 118,847 Jews deported
- **Summer 1942**: Additional deportations from Chernovtsy (4,200) and Dorohoi (450)
- **Total deported: approximately 200,000 Jews**

**Camp system:**
- Approximately **150 ghettos and camps** functioned in Transnistria
- Largest and most lethal: [[Bogdanovka]], [[Domanovka]], [[Acmecetca]]
- [[Pechora]] and [[Vapniarka]] concentration camps established winter 1941-1942
- Vapniarka reserved for Jewish political prisoners from Romania proper

**The Bogdanovka Massacre (December 1941 - January 1942):**
- Location: Bogdanovka camp, Transnistria
- **40,000-48,000 Jews murdered**
- Methods: shooting, burning alive
- 5,000 sick and maimed Jews locked in sheds and burned alive
- Remaining inmates shot over following months; bodies cremated

**Additional massacres:**
- **January-February 1942**: 18,000 Jews murdered at Domanevka and Akmechetka
- Perpetrators: Romanian gendarmes, Ukrainian policemen, local German units

### Death Toll in Transnistria

| Category | Estimated Deaths |
|----------|-----------------|
| Jews deported from Romania | 105,000-120,000 |
| Jews indigenous to Transnistria | 115,000-180,000 |
| **Total in Transnistria** | **220,000-300,000** |

### The Roma Genocide (Porajmos)

The Antonescu regime also targeted [[Roma people]] for deportation and extermination:

**Deportation process:**
- **25 May 1942**: Census of "problem Gypsies" conducted
- **June-August 1942**: "Nomadic" Roma deported
- **September 1942**: "Sedentary" Roma with criminal records deported
- Entire families deported - more than half were children

**Statistics:**
- **25,000-26,000 Roma deported** to Transnistria
- **11,000-36,000 Roma killed** (estimates vary)
- Deaths from hunger, cold, disease, and deprivation

> [!important] Romanian Initiative
> "Antonescu's deportation of the Roma people to Transnistria - where some 20,000 died of disease, starvation, and cold - was not a result of German pressure, but something he initiated on his own."

### End of Extermination Policy

The systematic killings ended in **October 1942** when Antonescu changed course:
- Military situation shifting against Axis
- Deportations halted by **April 1943**
- Approximately **375,000 Jews survived** in Wallachia, Moldavia, and southern Transylvania

> [!note] The Antonescu Paradox
> "He was an anti-Semite and yet, despite the deportations to Transnistria, more Jews survived under his rule than in any other country within Axis Europe. While up to 300,000 Jews were victims of Antonescu's policies, some 375,000 Jews are estimated to have survived."

## 7. Military Campaigns on the Eastern Front

### Operation Munchen: Recovery of Bessarabia (July 1941)

Romania's initial objective in joining [[Operation Barbarossa]] was recovering territories lost to the Soviet Union in 1940.

**The campaign:**
- **Code name**: Operation Munchen
- **Start date**: 2 July 1941
- **Forces**: Romanian 3rd Army (north) and 4th Army (south)
- **Strength**: 325,000 troops, 1,000 artillery pieces

**Progress:**
- **5 July**: Cernauti (capital of Northern Bukovina) seized
- **16 July**: Chisinau (Bessarabian capital) captured after heavy fighting
- **26 July**: Entire region under Romanian-German control
- **17 August**: Bessarabia and Northern Bukovina formally reintegrated into Romania

**Air operations:**
- 5,100 missions flown
- 88 enemy aircraft shot down in combat
- 108 destroyed on ground
- Romanian losses: 58 aircraft

### Beyond Bessarabia: The Advance into Soviet Territory

After **3 August 1941**, Romanian forces crossed the Dniester River into pre-1940 Soviet territory:

**Major operations:**
- [[Siege of Odessa]] (August-October 1941)
- [[Battle of Crimea]] (Spring-Summer 1942)
- [[Battle of the Caucasus]] (1942)
- [[Battle of Stalingrad]] (1942-1943)

**Romanian military contribution:**
- **Third-largest Axis army in Europe**
- Committed more troops to Eastern Front than all other German allies combined
- By September 1941: ~12% of German-led forces but suffered 30% of casualties
- **585,000 troops** deployed in assault on USSR (June-October 1941)

### The Siege of Odessa (August-October 1941)

Romanian Fourth Army besieged [[Odessa]] for two months:
- **16 October 1941**: City finally captured
- **Romanian casualties: over 80,000**
- Heavy losses effectively reduced Romanian offensive capability

### The Stalingrad Disaster (November 1942 - February 1943)

Two Romanian armies participated in the [[Battle of Stalingrad]]:

**Deployment:**
- [[Romanian Third Army]]: Protecting northern flank of German 6th Army
- [[Romanian Fourth Army]]: Protecting southern flank

**Operation Uranus (19 November 1942):**
Soviet offensive punched through both Romanian flanks, encircling German 6th Army.

**Romanian casualties at Stalingrad:**
- **109,000 casualties** (November-December 1942)
- **70,000 captured or missing**
- **250,000+ killed, wounded, captured, or missing** across 19 divisions
- Effectively ended Romanian offensive capability for remainder of war

### Total Military Casualties

| Category | Estimated Toll |
|----------|---------------|
| Fighting for Axis (1941-1944) | 350,000-400,000 |
| Fighting for Allies (1944-1945) | 170,000 |
| **Total Romanian military dead** | ~370,000 |
| Economic damage | Equal to 12 years of pre-war Romanian budgets |

## 8. Methods of Control and Governance

### Nature of the Dictatorship

Antonescu's regime was a military dictatorship with distinctive characteristics:

**Structure:**
- Title: Conducator ("Leader")
- Prime Minister with absolute powers
- King Michael I reduced to ceremonial role
- Not a one-party state (after Iron Guard suppression)

**Distinctive features:**
- Romania maintained sovereignty - never occupied by Wehrmacht
- Preserved certain political latitude for opposition critics
- "Perhaps the least servile among the German satellite governments"
- Relied on army rather than party apparatus

### Antisemitic Governance

The regime's defining policy was persecution of Jews:

**Key policies:**
- Forced labor for Jewish men aged 20-50
- "Romanianization" of economy - seizure of Jewish property and businesses
- Exclusion from professions
- Deportation to Transnistria of "enemy populations"
- Mass shootings and pogroms

**Property confiscation:**
- Jewish buildings nationalized (March 1941)
- **1,042 Jewish community buildings expropriated** (July 1942 - August 1944)
- Including temples, synagogues, and schools

### Relationship with Germany

Antonescu maintained Romania's status as ally rather than puppet:

**Key elements:**
- Personal respect from Hitler
- Control of Transnistria as Romanian-administered territory
- Independent initiative in Holocaust - not directed by SS
- Economic leverage through oil supply (40% of German oil by 1944)

## 9. Overthrow: The August 1944 Coup

### Background

By summer 1944, the strategic situation had become untenable:

- Heavy casualties at Stalingrad had decimated Romanian forces
- Soviet conquest of Romania appeared inevitable within months
- Hitler suspected Romania might seek separate peace
- Opposition politicians formed [[National Democratic Bloc]] (June 1944)

### The Coup (23 August 1944)

[[King Michael I]] led the coup with support from multiple political parties.

**The confrontation:**
- King Michael met with Ion Antonescu, Foreign Minister [[Mihai Antonescu]], and General [[Constantin Sanatescu]]
- Antonescu informed King of dire frontline situation
- King asked Antonescu to sign armistice with Allies and Soviets
- Antonescu refused, especially regarding Soviet Union
- King responded: "If things are so, then there's nothing we can do"

**The arrest:**
- A colonel and four soldiers entered and arrested the Prime Minister
- **10 PM, 23 August 1944**: King announced on radio that Antonescu was deposed
- Armistice with Allied Powers and Soviet Union would be accepted

### Political Support

The coup was backed by:
- [[Romanian Communist Party]]
- [[Social Democratic Party]]
- [[National Liberal Party]]
- [[National Peasants' Party]]

### Aftermath and Strategic Impact

**Immediate consequences:**
- Romanian Army declared unilateral ceasefire with Red Army
- New Prime Minister: Lt. Gen. [[Constantin Sanatescu]]
- Antonescu handed over to Romanian communists (1 September)
- Later transferred to Soviet custody

**Strategic significance:**
- Albert Speer called Romania's defection "decisive"
- Cut off German access to Romanian oil and Turkish chromium
- Hitler made first admission that the war was lost
- Bulgaria defected shortly after
- Intensified resistance in Greece and Yugoslavia

> [!quote] Churchill's Assessment
> Romania's defection "tore a huge hole in the Nazi's Balkan front."

**The Armistice:**
- **12 September 1944**: [[Moscow Armistice]] signed
- Romania formally switched to Allied side
- ~140,000 Romanian POWs taken by Soviets before armistice
- ~130,000 transported to Soviet camps; many perished

### Romania Fights for the Allies

From armistice to war's end:
- Romanian forces fought alongside Soviets
- Campaigns in Transylvania, Hungary, Czechoslovakia
- **May 1945**: Romanian 1st and 4th Armies participated in [[Prague Offensive]]
- **169,822 total casualties** fighting on Allied side

## 10. Trial and Execution

### The People's Tribunal

**Establishment:**
- Created by [[King Michael I]]'s decree on 21 April 1945
- Based on [[Nuremberg Trials]] model
- Four categories of indictments: conspiracy, crimes against peace, war crimes, crimes against humanity

### The Trial of Major War Criminals (May 1946)

The Antonescu group trial was the most prominent case:

**The defendants:**
- Ion Antonescu
- 23 former high-ranking officials (ministers, under-secretaries, officers, intelligence officials)
- 7 leaders of the [[Legion of the Archangel Michael]]

**Charges:**
- Treason in favor of Nazi Germany
- Anti-Soviet war
- The [[Iasi Pogrom]]
- Racial legislation
- Dispossession of Jews
- Deportation to Transnistria
- Mass executions in Odessa and Transnistria
- Internment in camps and ghettos

**Verdict (17 May 1946):**
- Antonescu and 12 associates convicted of war crimes
- Death sentences issued
- Appeals for clemency rejected by both Supreme Court and King Michael

### The Execution (1 June 1946)

**Those executed:**
- Ion Antonescu
- [[Mihai Antonescu]] (Foreign Minister, unrelated)
- [[Gheorghe Alexianu]] (Governor of Transnistria)
- [[Constantin Vasiliu]] (Deputy Minister of Internal Affairs)

**Method:** Military firing squad at Camp Jilava, near Bucharest

## 11. Legacy and Historical Reassessment

### Suppression Under Communism

During the Communist period (1947-1989):
- True history of Holocaust in Romania suppressed
- Few Romanians aware of extent of Antonescu's involvement
- Important Antonescu papers removed by Soviet authorities (1944-1945)

### Rehabilitation Attempts (Post-1989)

After the fall of Communism, efforts emerged to rehabilitate Antonescu:

**Origins:**
- 1974: Marin Preda's novel "Delirul" began reappraisal
- Post-1989: Nationalist groups rallied behind Antonescu's figure
- Bronze busts and memorials erected (e.g., outside Slobozia police headquarters)

**Revisionist arguments:**
- Focus on procedural errors of 1946 trial
- Cast Antonescu as "victim" of Communist takeover
- Portrayed as anti-Russian/anti-Communist rather than antisemitic
- Some attempted to credit regime with "saving" 400,000 surviving Jews

**Legal rehabilitation attempts (1995-2004):**
- Romanian Supreme Court rehabilitated some war criminals
- 1998: US officials (Senators D'Amato and Smith) blocked rehabilitation of 7 Antonescu government members
- Romania threatened with reassessment of NATO/EU support

### International Response

**Opposition to rehabilitation:**
- 15 US congressmen signed protest letter to President [[Ion Iliescu]]
- State Department urged Romania to "condemn political groups that attempt to resuscitate extreme-nationalist and anti-Semitic currents"
- [[Federation of Jewish Communities in Romania]] documented war crimes

> [!quote] Historian Andrei Pippidi
> "Personally, I am shocked to find that democratization of the country should start with the rehabilitation of a fascist dictator."

### The Wiesel Commission (2003-2004)

In response to statements by Romanian officials minimizing the Holocaust, President [[Ion Iliescu]] established the [[International Commission on the Holocaust in Romania]]:

**Leadership:**
- Chair: Nobel laureate [[Elie Wiesel]]
- Backed by American Jewish Committee, B'nai Brith, US Holocaust Memorial Museum, [[Yad Vashem]]

**Key findings (November 2004):**
- 280,000-380,000 Jews murdered under Romanian control
- Over 11,000 Roma killed
- "Ion Antonescu's regime...is responsible for the initiation and the organization of the repressive actions and the extermination measures"
- Rejected false narrative that credited Antonescu with saving surviving Jews

**Recommendations implemented:**
- [[National Holocaust Commemoration Day]] established (around October 9)
- Institute for the Study of the Holocaust in Romania (established 2005)
- Holocaust history included in public school curricula
- National monument to Romanian Holocaust victims

### Current Assessment

The Wiesel Commission report represents the definitive historical judgment:

> [!quote] Wiesel Commission Report
> "Undoubtedly, Germany's Nazi regime bears the main responsibility for the European Holocaust. But it is Ion Antonescu's regime that is responsible for the initiation and the organization of the repressive actions and the extermination measures directed against the Jews of Romania and the territories under Romanian administration."
